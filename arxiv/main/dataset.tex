\section{Dataset} % (fold)
\label{sec:dataset}


We consider data from the National Basketball Association (NBA). To access comprehensive game data, we utilize the Python package \texttt{nba\_api}\footnote{\url{https://github.com/swar/nba_api/}}, which provides access to the APIs of \url{nba.com}. The dataset consists of both made and missed field goal attempt locations from the offensive half-court from all NBA games spanning the seasons between $2018-2019$ and $2022-2024$. Filtering the dataset, we focus solely on players who have made more than $1000$ field goal attempts during this six-season period, resulting in a cohort of $173$ players. These players accounted for a total of $717381$ shots attempted, of which $340466$ ($47\%$) were successful. Subsequently, we exclude shots deemed impossible (e.g., out-of-bounds), leaving us with a dataset comprising $715734$ shots. \textcolor{red}{This results in a cohort of $\dots$ players, with $\dots$ attempted shots and $\dots$ ($\dots$) made shots.}

\textcolor{red}{To analyze shooting behavior, we employ a 2-dimensional kernel density estimation for both the attempted and made shots, utilizing Silverman's rule \cite{silvermanDensityEstimationStatistics1986} for bandwidth estimation. The density estimation is conducted on a regularly spaced grid consisting of $201 \times 201$ points (see Figure \ref{fig:shoots_make_miss} for the estimated densities for Stephen Curry). We obtain observations of bivariate functional data (i.e., $P = 2$ functional features), where both features are defined on a two-dimensional rectangular domain, and observed on an equidistant grid of size $201 \times 201$.}
